Angel Abimael — “El Ángel Dorado”

I’m from San Diego, California, proudly Oaxaqueño. Music and dance have been my way to express both hard moments and pure happiness. I started singing as a kid; folklórico pushed me to take performing seriously. Since then, I’ve blended voice, movement, and culture to tell stories that feel honest and joyful.

Bilingual performer Folklórico roots Trumpet • Guitar • Violin

Instruments & Skills

  • Primary: trumpet, guitar, violin
  • Also explored: French horn, flute, piccolo, clarinet
  • Singer • Dancer (folklórico) • Performer

Sound & Performance

  • Genres I explore: romantic, pop, hip‑hop, opera
  • Style: charming, fun, energetic, emotional, and audience‑first

Artists I admire

Mon Laferte Kali Uchis Las Marías
Artistic vision: I want people to love themselves, have fun, and feel free. Whether it’s a pop moment or a heartfelt ballad, I aim to spark joy and connection — the kind you feel in your body.
Short‑term: perform more, release polished live clips, and keep developing my sound.
Long‑term: build a lasting career on stage and in the studio — and one day teach folklórico and the arts.
Dreams: collaborate on major campaigns (inspired by GAP’s artist features), be a fashion house ambassador, and design my own brand.

What makes my music different

Charm and passion. I bring dance energy and open‑heart storytelling to every show so people can feel happiness and authenticity in the moment.

What might surprise you

My journey has included a big glow‑up — growing, learning, and finding confidence. I want fans to see that I’m a regular person who chased a dream, and that anyone can build a better version of themselves.

Beyond the stage

I love cooking, gaming, fashion, parties, and new experiences — from modern huapangos to trying things that get my adrenaline going. I deal with anxiety too, and I’m learning to channel it into creativity.

What music means to me

Music is culture and emotion. It carries history and community forward. I want to honor my roots and help people feel seen — now and for the future.

“Always dream. Without a future to imagine, we’d be stuck in the past.”

For my community

I’m proud of my Oaxacan heritage — our language, food, and faith traditions. I want to share that love with the world and uplift others who see themselves in my story.
